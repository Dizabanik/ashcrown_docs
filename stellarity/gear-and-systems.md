# Gear & Systems

## <mark style="color:orange;">Armor Sets</mark>

Stellarity adds 4 main post-Dragon armor paths.

All of them start from Netherite Armor and require an Enderite Smithing Template.

### Hallowed Armor

Built for speed, jumps, safer falls, and evasive play.

The full set unlocks **Holy Protection**, which can cancel incoming damage, grant brief immunity, and trigger strong mobility buffs on a cooldown.

### Shulker Armor

Built for tanking and anti-swarm defense.

It gives very high toughness and knockback resistance, but lowers movement and attack speed.

The full set can spawn friendly Shulker Bullets, reduce damage in crowds, and grant immunity to Levitation and Wither.

### Champion Armor

The damage-focused set.

It lowers toughness, then trades that defense for more attack speed and higher damage output.

### Floral Armor

A sustain-focused set.

It supports lifesteal and constant healing during extended combat.

## <mark style="color:orange;">Signature Weapons</mark>

### Dragonblade

A legendary sword tied to the first dragon kill.

It applies Dragon Stacks on hit. At 3 stacks, you can crouch to launch the target away with a powerful warp punch.

Wall impacts massively increase the damage.

### Kaleidoscope

A greataxe from the Empress of Light.

It charges while held, then releases a fire-based area blast on attack.

Higher charge means more damage, more burn time, and more armor penetration.

### Tamaris

A fast corrupted Netherite blade with high attack speed.

### Prismatic Punch

A fast ranged weapon dropped by the Empress.

It performs especially well against large or fast targets.

### Starless Scythe

A sweeping polearm with built-in healing and farming utility.

### Zepha Sword

A rare End City weapon that boosts movement speed while held.

### Frost Harvester

A scaling weapon from Frozen Spikes enemies.

It grows stronger as it claims kills.

## <mark style="color:orange;">Void Fishing</mark>

Void Fishing lets you cast into open air far from the central island.

This creates an End-based resource economy and removes some dependence on Overworld or Nether farming.

### Rules

* Catch time is usually 15 to 45 seconds.
* Lure reduces the wait time.
* The Fisher of Voids reduces it even further.
* Loot depends on the biome where you cast.

### Notable Biome Loot

* **The Hallow:** Prismite, Starlight Soot, Cherry Leaves.
* **Amethyst Forest:** Amethyst fish and crystal resources.
* **Fiery Hills:** Glowstone Dust, Blaze Rods, and magma materials.
* **Prismarine Forest:** Prismarine blocks, crystals, and Glow Ink Sacs.
* **Original End biomes:** Chorus resources and Eyes of Ender.

Rare global loot includes Enderite Smithing Templates, Winged Keys, strong enchanted books, and the Fisher of Voids itself.

## <mark style="color:orange;">Utility Systems</mark>

### Hallow Transmutation

The Hallow's magenta water can convert certain dropped items into other materials.

Many conversions work both ways, which creates an alternate farming route.

### Trinkets

Important utility items include:

* **Soaring Insignia:** boosts Elytra mobility and enables near-limitless flight.
* **Dragon's Eye:** affects Endermen and Endermites, useful for farms.
* **Satchel of Voids:** expands inventory capacity.
* **Books of Jinx, Light, and Updraft:** magical utility tomes.

### Void Safety

A Totem of Undying can save players from void deaths by teleporting them upward and applying Slow Falling.

Loyalty items, like Tridents, also return safely instead of being lost to the void.

### Alchemy And Food

Lingering Potions are much stronger than vanilla in Stellarity.

The pack also adds biome-linked food like Sushi, Golden Chorus Fruit, Candied Chorus Fruit, Frozen Carpaccio, and Chorus Stew.
