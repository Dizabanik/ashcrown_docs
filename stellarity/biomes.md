# Biomes

## <mark style="color:orange;">Biome Overview</mark>

Stellarity adds 15 new End biomes and reworks the 4 vanilla ones.

The terrain is far more vertical than vanilla, with tall peaks, chasms, broken island chains, and thicker floating landmasses.

Biomes fall into 3 broad groups:

* **Barren:** sparse, hostile, and resource-poor.
* **Lively:** dense, magical, or safer zones with stronger local identity.
* **Transition:** mixed biomes that connect both styles.

## <mark style="color:orange;">Major Biomes</mark>

### The Hallow

A bright magical biome inspired by Terraria.

* End Stone is replaced by Diorite and Calcite.
* Features rainbow trees, blue grass, magenta water, and magenta fog.
* Pixies spawn here and drop Pixie Dust, also called Starlight Soot.
* The Chapel of Light only generates here.

### Amethyst Forest

A crystal-heavy biome built around vertical amethyst growth.

* Large crystal formations dominate the landscape.
* Huge amethyst stalactites hang below floating terrain.
* Strong source of amethyst-related materials.

### Flesh Tundra

A hostile biome with organic terrain and grotesque visuals.

* The ground looks living and corrupted.
* Includes adapted hostile mobs and biome-specific loot.

### Prismarine Forest

One of the safest biomes in the pack.

* Hostile mob spawning is suppressed here.
* Good place to regroup, sort inventory, and prepare routes.

### Endless Dunes

A dry wasteland with signs of earlier failed expeditions.

* Can generate Desert Ruins.
* Can also generate grounded Shipwrecks.

## <mark style="color:orange;">Other Biomes</mark>

### Fiery Hills

A volcanic biome with strong heat-themed generation.

### Frozen Spikes

An icy biome that changes movement feel and adds heavy traversal friction.

### Prismatic Dunes

A dune biome with stronger color-driven terrain identity.

### End Wilds

A rough wilderness-style biome for open exploration.

### Sculk Growth

A sculk-infected biome that brings a darker tone into the End.

## <mark style="color:orange;">Exploration Notes</mark>

* Falling is a bigger threat than in vanilla because islands no longer share one safe height.
* Biome choice matters for farming, fishing, and boss preparation.
* The Hallow and Prismarine Forest are two of the most valuable utility biomes.

{% hint style="info" %}
If you need a safer route or a staging point, aim for a Prismarine Forest before pushing deeper into hostile outer islands.
{% endhint %}
