# Getting Started

## <mark style="color:orange;">Content</mark>

This is the Dark Souls Death (RIP) section.

Dark Souls Death adds a soul at your death location and saves your last death coordinates.

Each death reduces your maximum health. Recovering your soul restores that lost heart.

It works well for survival servers, harder worlds, and co-op runs where death should matter.

## <mark style="color:orange;">Main Features</mark>

* Spawn your soul on death.
* Keep souls active for `1` hour by default, as long as the chunk stays loaded.
* Lose `1` maximum heart on each death.
* Recover `1` heart for each soul you reclaim.
* Let only the owner recover their own soul.
* Grant a short regeneration effect after soul recovery.
* Show your death position when you die.
* Show your death position again with `/trigger rip`.
* Protect nearby dropped items in a `16` block radius.
* Play a visual animation when a player gets close.

## <mark style="color:orange;">How It Works</mark>

{% stepper %}
{% step %}
### Die and spawn a soul

When you die, the datapack creates a soul at your death location.

Your latest death coordinates are also saved.
{% endstep %}

{% step %}
### Lose a heart

Each death removes `1` maximum heart.

This makes repeated deaths more punishing until you recover your souls.
{% endstep %}

{% step %}
### Find your death location

Check the coordinates shown on death.

You can also use `/trigger rip` later to display them again.
{% endstep %}

{% step %}
### Recover your soul

Return to your own soul and reclaim it.

You get back `1` maximum heart and a short regeneration effect.
{% endstep %}
{% endstepper %}

{% hint style="info" %}
Souls also protect dropped items around them within `16` blocks.
{% endhint %}
